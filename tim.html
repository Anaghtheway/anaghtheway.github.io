Thermal Interface Materials (TIM)

Thermal Interface Materials bridge the microscopic air gaps between a heat source and its cooling solution. Without a good TIM, even the best cold plate can't do its job — air gaps create massive thermal resistance.

Types of TIMs

  • Thermal Paste (Grease) – Most common. Silicone or metal-oxide based compounds. Typical conductivity: 1–12 W/m·K.
  • Thermal Pads – Pre-cut solid pads for gap filling. Easy to apply, lower performance than paste.
  • Phase-Change Materials – Solid at room temp, soften at operating temp to fill gaps. Good long-term reliability.
  • Liquid Metal – Gallium-based alloys. Extremely high conductivity (20–70 W/m·K) but electrically conductive — risky near components.
  • Indium Foil – Soft metal that conforms under clamping pressure. Used in high-reliability applications.

Key Properties

  • Thermal conductivity (W/m·K)
  • Bond-line thickness (BLT)
  • Pump-out and dry-out resistance over time
  • Electrical conductivity (important for liquid metals)
  • Application pressure and clamping force

TIM in the Thermal Stack

TIM sits between the chip's integrated heat spreader (IHS) and the cold plate or heat sink. In some designs, there are two TIM layers — TIM1 between the die and IHS, and TIM2 between the IHS and cooler.
