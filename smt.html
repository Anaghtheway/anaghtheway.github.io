Electronic Packaging & SMT

The foundation of the thermal stack — the substrate, PCB, and solder joints that connect the chip to the rest of the system. Packaging affects both electrical performance and thermal pathways.

Surface Mount Technology (SMT)

  • BGA (Ball Grid Array) – Solder balls connect the chip package to the PCB. Widely used for processors and FPGAs.
  • LGA (Land Grid Array) – Contact pads with spring-loaded pins in the socket. Common in server CPUs.
  • Flip Chip – Die is flipped and directly bonded to the substrate via micro-bumps.
  • Wire Bonding – Traditional method using thin gold/copper wires. Still used in many IC packages.

Thermal Considerations in Packaging

  • Solder joint thermal resistance and reliability under thermal cycling
  • Substrate material (organic vs. ceramic) and its thermal conductivity
  • Thermal vias in PCBs for heat conduction to bottom layers
  • Underfill materials to reduce stress and improve thermal contact
  • CTE mismatch between die, substrate, and PCB causing warpage

Advanced Packaging

Technologies like TSMC's CoWoS, Intel's EMIB, and 2.5D/3D packaging create new thermal challenges with multiple dies on a single interposer, requiring innovative cooling approaches at the package level.
